# Legal and Forensic Operating Boundary

Clarity Structures Webapp is not positioned as generic legal advice or automatic forensic certification by default.

It is an operational evidence and decision-traceability system designed to generate structured, verifiable and auditable case records.

The system may support legal, compliance or forensic workflows, but the legal or expert value of an output depends on the operating mode, review protocol and professional responsibility attached to that output.

## Default mode: operational evidence system

By default, the system provides:

- structured intake capture;
- consent-aware contact and case submission;
- deterministic decision routing;
- versioned decision logic;
- audit trail generation;
- administrative review support;
- evidence-oriented metadata;
- traceable operational records.

In this mode, the system does not replace legal judgment, expert human review or formal forensic certification.

## Legal review mode

Legal advice may only be provided when the system output is reviewed, validated and signed by a qualified legal professional under a specific engagement.

In this mode, the platform acts as an evidence, intake, routing and documentation layer.

The legal conclusion belongs to the qualified professional, not to the software itself.

Required elements:

- identified legal reviewer;
- defined client or matter scope;
- reviewed source material;
- versioned output;
- date and time of review;
- human validation;
- final signed or approved legal position.

## Forensic / expert certification mode

Forensic or expert certification may only be issued when the evidence bundle is produced under a defined protocol and signed by an identified expert.

In this mode, the system may support:

- evidence capture;
- hash calculation;
- bundle export;
- custody metadata;
- event chronology;
- technical reconstruction;
- verification material;
- appendices for expert review.

The certification is not created by the software alone.

It is created by the combination of:

- protocol;
- evidence source;
- technical method;
- chain-of-custody information;
- identified expert;
- scope and limitations;
- signed expert conclusion.

## Required expert bundle metadata

A forensic or expert bundle should include, at minimum:

- matter or case identifier;
- bundle generation timestamp;
- evidence source description;
- file names and references;
- SHA-256 and/or SHA-512 hashes;
- relevant event chronology;
- system version or commit reference;
- decision engine version, if applicable;
- export operator;
- review status;
- limitations;
- expert identity, when signed;
- signature or approval reference.

## Strategic positioning

The platform should not claim that every generated output is legal advice or forensic certification.

The stronger claim is:

> Clarity Structures Webapp generates structured operational evidence that can be reviewed, validated and, where appropriate, converted into legal or expert-certified outputs under a defined professional protocol.

That distinction protects the product while preserving the premium value of legal and forensic workflows.

## Operating principle

The system does not ask users to trust memory.

It creates records that can be reconstructed.

If a decision, event or document cannot be reconstructed, it should not be treated as operational evidence.
